Efficient Graph SLAM For Sparse Sensing

close

Abstract

Small, agile, and cheap nano drones demonstrate promising potentials to carry out dangerous indoor exploration missions, in which simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM) plays a vital role in mapping out unknown spaces and aiding autonomous navigation. However, state-of-the-art solutions for SLAM are tailored for dense and accurate sensors such as laser range-finders (LiDARs), which are uneconomical to be mounted on nano drones due to their limited battery and carrying capacity. Although some priors work has addressed SLAM with sparse sensing problems, they use filtering-based approaches that cannot solve the full SLAM problem. They are either making strong assumptions about the environment or computationally expensive. This work presents a system that uses line segment features with raw odometry to derive constraints in a pose graph, which are subsequently optimized with loop closures. Our experiments show that our method can construct 2D maps with 100 times real-time performance. Furthermore, the maps constructed by our algorithm have superior quality compared to prior works on sparse sensing and are comparable to maps produced with dense sensing.
